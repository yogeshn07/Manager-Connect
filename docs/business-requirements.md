# Business Requirements

## Problem Statement

Managers within the organization operate in silos. There is no dedicated space for them to build peer relationships, organize shared activities, celebrate each other, or stay connected beyond formal work channels. This leads to low manager cohesion, reduced morale, and limited cross-functional trust.

## Business Objectives

| ID | Objective | Success Metric |
|----|-----------|----------------|
| BO1 | Increase manager peer engagement | 80%+ monthly active usage among enrolled managers |
| BO2 | Drive organized social and wellness activities | Minimum 2 activities or events organized per month |
| BO3 | Build a culture of peer recognition | At least 1 recognition posted per week per active user |
| BO4 | Improve manager belonging and retention | Qualitative NPS improvement at 6-month review |
| BO5 | Provide a private, trusted community space | Zero unauthorized access incidents |

## Stakeholders

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Sponsoring Leader | Approves the investment and defines the community scope |
| Platform Admin | Manages users, content moderation, and app configuration |
| Manager Community (End Users) | Primary users of all features |
| IT / Security | Approves infrastructure choice and security posture |
| HR (Passive Awareness) | Informed but not operators; no access to platform data |

## Business Rules

- **BR1:** Only organization-approved managers may access the platform.
- **BR2:** The platform admin may invite, suspend, or remove users at any time.
- **BR3:** Content is visible only to platform members — no public sharing.
- **BR4:** The platform must not collect or expose performance-related data.
- **BR5:** User data may not be used for HR or disciplinary purposes under any circumstances.
- **BR6:** Activities and outings organized via the platform are voluntary and non-mandatory.
- **BR7:** The admin cannot view private direct messages between members.

## Value Proposition

Manager Connect delivers a low-friction, mobile-first space where managers can:
- Stay socially connected without relying on work email or general company channels.
- Organize and participate in voluntary wellness and social activities.
- Recognize each other's contributions in a non-hierarchical, peer-to-peer manner.
- Feel a sense of belonging within the management community.

## Business Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Low adoption if UX is poor | Medium | High | Invest in onboarding experience and push notification strategy |
| Privacy concerns from managers | Low | High | Clear data charter; explicit no-HR-use policy |
| Admin burden kills momentum | Medium | Medium | Lightweight moderation; self-organizing defaults |
| Platform perceived as surveillance | Low | High | Transparent data policy; remove any analytics that feel intrusive |
| Key organizer leaves and platform goes quiet | Medium | Medium | Enable multiple community organizer roles |
